With her influence spanning various social media platforms, including her own YouTube channel and OnlyFans, Kopf has extended her reach to every available medium, although not without encountering some drama along the way. But after all, who is the modern influencer and creator who hasn’t been known for an entire web of controversies?

Since her beginnings on Twitch, the model slash influencer slash streamer slash adult entertainer has been involved in a fair share of troubles – allow us to list them and introduce her to you.

Who is Corinna Kopf?

Known as the “pouty girl,” on social media, Corinna Kopf has amassed a staggering 6.8 million followers on Instagram, where she consistently shares updates with her dedicated fanbase. In addition to her Instagram success, Kopf has also gained a significant following on platforms like Twitter, Twitch, and OnlyFans. While Kopf keeps her personal life relatively private, there are some essential details that any devoted fan should know about this popular streamer, and here is everything you need to know.

Born on December 1, 1995, in Palatine, Illinois, the 26-year-old model first started to gain popularity on YouTube for being part of David Dobrik’s squad in 2016. Known for doing risky and even life-threatening pranks, Dobrik’s channel had a miscellaneous assortment of content.

Over the course of time, Dobrik’s squad members started leaving the group one by one with the number of controversies and allegations against the YouTuber continuously arising. To this day, Kopf is one of the few remaining members of the squad who defends Dobrik, crediting him as the person who opened her doors to fame. 

However, since shifting to creating gaming content, Corinna has barely made an appearance in Dobrik’s vlogs, focusing on her career on Twitch instead. Nevertheless, her streaming career has proven to have had a rocky start from the second she joined Twitch, thus starting her long and conflictual battle with the streaming platform until this day.

Why was Corinna Kopf banned from Twitch and where is she now?

In 2019, the influencer received her first ban from the streaming platform, immediately signing a contract with Facebook Gaming instead. During that time, Corinna’s Fortnite streams did incredibly well, and after two years, Kopf has finally made her way into Twitch again, hopeful for some improvement in her relationship with the platform. 

Corinna Kopf’s second ban

Upon making “just talking,” and gaming videos, Kopf’s popularity exponentially increased on the streaming platform, with her videos amounting to a huge number of viewers as well as followers. However, that was all cut short when in May, just two months after joining back on the platform, Corinna received a 24-hour ban on Twitch, for having “inappropriate attire.” This ban, although short-lived, has once again damaged the streamer’s relationship with the platform, who took to Twitter to share her frustration.

How Corinna Kopf responded to the Twitch ban

The streamer would go on to humorously add emojis to the caption “ain’t the first time”, referring to her 2019 ban, which was caused by a two-year resurfaced video. During this time, however, the model has kept busy with her active Instagram presence, as well as her main source of income, OnlyFans. 

At the time of writing, Kopf’s Twitch channel is now up with the most recent videos dating back to early June of 2023. Even though her channel has still been re-established, in February 2023, Dexerto announced the streamer would now be holding her live streams on Kick, alongside the former-biggest star on Twitch, xQc.

What is Corinna Kopf’s net worth?

Luckily, the ban has not affected Kopf’s value. When it comes to the model’s net worth, everything seems to be positively looking up. CelebrityNetWorth has concluded Corinna’s net worth to be $12 million as of 2023. In August 2021, Kopf admitted to earning over $4 million on OnlyFans alone, and if it is indeed true then that would make the streamer one of the highest-paid creators on the adult platform. On Logan Paul’s Impaulsive podcast, Kopf has also admitted that she still earns over $1 million on OnlyFans. 

Since then, fans have been able to keep up with the influencer on social media and OnlyFans, as her latest streams were made nearly a month ago, which is not surprising when taking into consideration how displeased a creator would be to have their content unfairly banned.
